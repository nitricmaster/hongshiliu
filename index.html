About Me

I am a graduate student in Applied Economics at the University of Maryland, College Park. I hold a B.S. in Economics from Wenzhou-Kean University, China.

My work spans econometric modeling, financial economics, and data science. I have hands-on experience applying panel data methods, fixed-effects regression, and machine learning workflows in Python and R to real-world economic questions — from the impact of ICT on U.S. productivity to green credit policy in China.

I also completed a summer internship at the Industrial and Commercial Bank of China, where I worked on credit-risk analysis and client data management.
