About Me

I am an economist and computer scientist. My research focuses on search and matching, behavioral optimal stopping problems, financial economics, and data science.

I hold a PhD in Economics from University College London, where my dissertation was titled "Essays on Regret, Reputation and Search." I completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Aalto University in Finland and served as an assistant professor at Universidad Torcuato di Tella in Buenos Aires. I currently teach Financial Economics and R Programming at the University of Maryland, College Park, and provide consulting services to international organizations including the World Bank Group.

Research Interests

Search & Matching

Modeling how agents search for partners in matching markets, and how experience and uncertainty shape selectivity and behavior.

Optimal Stopping

Examining stopping decisions under regret and uncertainty, uncovering non-monotonic patterns in agent behavior.

Financial Economics

Applying economic theory to financial markets, including reputation dynamics under competition and asset pricing.

Data Science

Leveraging quantitative methods and programming in R, Matlab, and C++ to analyze economic data and test theoretical predictions.
