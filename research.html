Unraveling the Interplay Between ICT and Economic Development: A Case Study of the United States

Sept 2023 – Dec 2023

Objective: Investigated how the diffusion of ICT influences U.S. economic development and productivity growth.

  • Completed a panel dataset (Excel-based) of 51 U.S. states (2015–2022) using official Census and BEA data covering ICT, income, and demographic variables.
  • Applied two-way fixed-effects regression in Python to model the impact of digitalization on real GDP.
  • Processed and visualized empirical results, confirming that ICT investment significantly promotes regional economic development.

The Effect of Green Credit Policy on Diminishing Environment Pollution in China

Sept 2023 – Dec 2023

Objective: Assessed the impact of China's green credit policies on reducing industrial air pollution and promoting sustainability.

  • Built a provincial panel dataset (2000–2020) from the China Statistical Yearbook, Energy Statistical Yearbook, and Environment Statistical Yearbook, integrating economic, financial, and environmental indicators.
  • Applied a panel regression with two-way fixed effects in Python to estimate how the Green Credit Index, investment, and regulation affect pollution levels.
  • Verified multicollinearity and model consistency, confirming that green credit significantly contributes to pollution mitigation.

Trade-off Between Inequality and Poverty: Examples From the U.S.

Feb 2022 – May 2022

Objective: Explored whether a trade-off exists between inequality reduction and poverty alleviation in the U.S. from 2000 to 2020.

  • Collected U.S. data (2000–2020) on poverty rate, Gini index, and GDP per capita from World Bank and U.S. Census Bureau sources.
  • Conducted correlation and regression analyses in Excel to evaluate the trade-off relationship between inequality and poverty.
  • Compared absolute and relative measures of inequality to identify differences in their association with economic growth.

The Effect of Household Poverty on Children: Examples From WKU

Feb 2022 – May 2022

Objective: Examined how household economic status affects children's educational performance and psychological well-being.

  • Collected primary data through a 200-participant survey at WKU, covering family income, academic ratings, mental health, and nutrition.
  • Utilized descriptive and regression analyses to quantify the relationship between poverty levels and child development outcomes.
