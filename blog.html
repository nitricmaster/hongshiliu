Working Papers

Learning While Searching

I model the search behavior of uncertain agents seeking partners in matching markets. Positive experiences (many past offers) lead the agents to act more selectively. I show that agents are more selective when accepting offers than when proposing them.

Status: Working paper

Reputation and Competition

I extend Board and Meyer-Ter-Vehn's reputation model by incorporating Bertrand competition. Competition strictly reduces the level of reputation the firms find worth keeping, resulting in lower quality and prices compared to monopoly scenarios.

Status: Working paper

Optimal Stopping with Regret

I examine optimal stopping decisions when agents anticipate future regret. Stopping occurs later than in a model without regret, though stopping behavior displays surprising non-monotonic patterns relative to key parameters.

Status: Working paper

Optimal Search and the Maximum Process

I analyze an impatient agent's choice between searching (receiving Brownian motion payoffs) or stopping to claim accumulated maximums. The paper offers closed-form solutions for stopping time distributions with applications to creative pursuits.

Status: Working paper
